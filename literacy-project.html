Traditional EdTech Fails the Marginalized.

Zero Grid Electricity

Consistent power to charge high-end devices is virtually non-existent in target camp environments.

Zero Broadband Connectivity

Cloud-based learning platforms and video streaming are inaccessible without reliable internet infrastructure.

Exclusionary Hardware Costs

Standard laptops and personal tablets are prohibitively expensive for marginalized families.

"Across the FCT and rural Nigeria, thousands of displaced children are locked out of education. We don't rely on infrastructure that isn't there. We build for the reality of the camp."

The iLearn Literacy Toolkit

1. "Offline-First" Pre-loaded Tablets

High-durability hardware preloaded with compressed, culturally resonant audio-visual Foundational Literacy and Numeracy (FLN) lessons. Requires zero data costs and zero internet connection to function.

2. The "Zero-Teacher" Playbook

Decentralizing the classroom. We empower parents and camp officials to become "Micro-Educators" using structured routines, easy-to-follow diagrams, and localized micro-coaching prompts.

3. The SMS Nudge Engine

Asynchronous tracking and capacity building via basic USSD and SMS. This creates a continuous feedback loop and allows us to send teaching nudges to Micro-Educators without broadband.
